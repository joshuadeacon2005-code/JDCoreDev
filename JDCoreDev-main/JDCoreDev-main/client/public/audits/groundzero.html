Overview

Ground Zero is stocked at Lane Crawford, Farfetch, Harvey Nichols, and a network of international multi-brand retailers. Brothers Eric and Philip Chu have been building this brand since 2003, and it has genuine critical standing — Highsnobiety features, international wholesale, LVMH Prize proximity. A brand with real institutional weight.

Their own website is functionally empty. The homepage renders a free-shipping banner and a login prompt. No products are visible above the fold. No brand story. No imagery. No navigation context. One of Hong Kong's most internationally recognised independent fashion labels is represented online by something that looks like a half-finished store template that was never actually launched.

Key Issues Found
Critical
Homepage is effectively blank. A login prompt and a free-shipping bar constitute the entire above-the-fold experience. The site appears broken to first-time visitors.
Critical
A brand active since 2003 with two decades of collections, press coverage, and collaborations has zero archival presence on its own website. This represents an enormous squandered asset.
Critical
Retail partners (Farfetch, Lane Crawford) are more effective storefronts for Ground Zero than the brand's own domain. Direct sales and brand equity are being ceded entirely to third parties.
Significant
No brand manifesto, no designer profiles, no design philosophy. The 'Zero Could Be Everything' brand concept — rich creative territory — is completely unexplored online.
Significant
Press coverage from Highsnobiety and international outlets exists but is not referenced or archived on site. Social proof at this level should be front and centre.
JD CoreDev Recommendation

Ground Zero's existing wholesale success means a full e-commerce rebuild may not be the immediate priority — but a brand presence site absolutely is. The pitch is a designer brand site: collection archive, campaign photography, press coverage, and a brand story that does justice to 20+ years of output.

Add a direct shop for exclusive drops or colourways unavailable at retail partners, capturing demand from customers who find the brand on Farfetch and want to buy direct. Ground Zero is leaving significant margin on the table every day their site sits empty.
